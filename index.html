Hello. I'm Miles Z. Sterrett. I'm a programmer. I usually program web apps. Check some of 'em out.

 
holscher products, inc

Holscher was in need of a new website, as well as new website managers. Requiring a catalog that could handle the demands of their customers, Holscher's site was no small project. They received a catalog module to add on to the already powerful functionality of HydraCMS, customized to their exacting needs. They love it.

 
red panda press

Red Panda Press was just getting started. They needed a quality web front for their printing business, including a full-featured Request For Quote form, customer management, and file management. Dave Strus and myself got to work building a custom site powered by Ruby on Rails, custom-fitted to Red Panda's business.

 
chacha underground

The site that ChaCha's thousands of guides use to view their activity, view their earnings, and pay themselves out had seen better days. Written in 2006 with a very early version of Rails, it was long in the tooth fora modern web app. A full rewrite to the latest version of Rails (in the spring of 2011) led to a more performant and stable site, and happier users.

 
indy hackers

IndyHackers.org helps Indianapolis-area programmers keep track of user groups and other related events in the area, and connects passionate programmers with local jobs. To handle the job board as well as the calendar that is central to IndyHackers, a Rails app was built that allows users to easily submit jobs, and allows me to easily manage them.

 
tell my politician

TellMyPolitician.com is a Rails app created to help citizens of the United States more easily find their congressional representatives' contact info. It accesses data provided by the Sunlight Foundation, champions of transparent government, to return addresses, phone numbers, and email addresses of Senators and Representatives.

 
chacha.me

ChaCha endeavored to build a social network upon their unique questions-and-answers concept, and they wanted it fast! ChaCha.me was born, built using Ruby on Rails and copious amounts of Javascript, integrating with Twitter and Facebook. ChaCha.me is geared to asking your friends questions, allowing you to do so publicly, privately, anonymously, or in mass quantities!
